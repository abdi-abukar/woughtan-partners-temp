Why Africa

The Investment Case: Africa's Time is Now

Sub-Saharan Africa represents one of the world's last high-growth frontiers — a region of 1.2 billion people with rapidly expanding middle classes, accelerating digital adoption, and improving business environments.

Five Key Investment Drivers

01

Demographic Dividend

Africa's population projected to reach 2.5 billion by 2050, with a median age of just 19 years. This young, urban population drives consumption growth and digital adoption.

02

Digital Leapfrogging

With over 600 million mobile money accounts, Africa leads in financial inclusion innovation, creating opportunities in fintech, e-commerce, and digital services.

03

Regional Integration: AfCFTA

The African Continental Free Trade Area creates a single market of 1.2 billion people with combined GDP of $2.5 trillion, showing 15% increase in intra-African trade.

04

Natural Resource Wealth

Africa holds 30% of global mineral reserves, 10% of oil reserves, and vast renewable energy potential. Energy transition creates opportunities in solar, wind, and green hydrogen.

05

Improving Business Environment

Reforms across multiple countries improve ease of doing business. Ghana, Rwanda, Kenya, and Côte d'Ivoire lead in creating investor-friendly environments.
